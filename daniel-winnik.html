Career

  • Winnik entered the University of New Hampshire of Hockey East after playing for the Wexford Raiders for two seasons in the Ontario Provincial Junior A Hockey League (OPJHL).
  • The Phoenix Coyotes selected Winnik in round 265 of the 2004 NHL Entry Draft after his freshman year. In his second season with the Wildcats, he began to find his scoring touch, with 18 goals for 40 points, and was one of UNH’s top players, earning a spot on the NCAA Northeast Regional All-Tournament team.
  • As the Wildcats’ top assist provider and the fifth-highest scorer in the Hockey East with 41 points, Daniel built on his breakout season in his junior season. He was chosen for the Hockey East Second All-Star Team and was awarded the league’s player of the month for November.
  • On March 31, 2006, he signed a three-year entry-level deal with the Coyotes after finishing a three-year stint with New Hampshire.
  • To conclude the 2005–06 season, he promptly made his professional debut with San Antonio Rampage, an AHL affiliate.
  • Winnik played a checking position with the Rampage in his first full professional season in 2006–07, contributing 21 points in 66 games while also spending time with the Rampage’s secondary affiliate, the Phoenix RoadRunners, in the ECHL.
  • In the 2007–08 season, Winnik was drafted by the Coyotes right out of training camp, and on October 4, 2007, in a 3-2 victory against the St. Louis Blues, he scored his first NHL goal.
  • Winnik had become a dependable member of the Coyotes squad by the conclusion of his first NHL season, and coach Wayne Gretzky regarded him as the group’s best penalty taker.
  • Dan had a sophomore slump in 2008–09, recording only 7 points in 49 games after a strong start to his NHL career. He was sent to San Antonio on January 1, 2009, for a five-game conditioning stint, despite seldom being utilized as a backup forward.
  • Winnik swiftly bounced back in the next 2009–10 season, reclaiming a checking line position and rediscovering his scoring touch, matching his career best with 15 assists. He was subsequently re-signed to an arbitrated one–year deal on July 24. He made his playoff debut against the Detroit Red Wings as a member of a resurgent Coyotes squad, playing in 7 games without recording a point.
  • The Coyotes traded Winnik to the Colorado Avalanche on June 28, 2010, in exchange for a fourth-round choice in the 2012 NHL Entry Draft.
  • On July 2, 2010, the Avalanche agreed to a two-year deal with him.
  • Winnik was exchanged by the Colorado Avalanche for Jamie McGinn, prospects Mike Connolly and Michael Sgarbossa, TJ Galiardi, and a seventh-round selection on February 27, 2012, at the trade deadline for the 2011–12 NHL season.
  • On July 20, 2012, Winnik signed a two-year free agent contract with division rivals, the Anaheim Ducks, after he and the Sharks were unable to come to terms on a new contract.
  • Winnik signed a $1.3 million one-year free agency deal with his hometown club, the Toronto Maple Leafs, on July 28, 2014.
  • Winnik demonstrated his versatility for the Maple Leafs in the 2014–15 campaign, contributing 25 points in 58 games while playing on all four lines and displaying strong penalty killing skills.
  • He was dealt to the Pittsburgh Penguins on February 25, 2015, with the Maple Leafs already out of the playoff race, for Zach Sill, a 2015 fourth-round selection, and a 2016 second-round pick. Winnik played in 21 games with the Penguins, recording 2 goals and 9 points, but he was unable to lead them to a successful postseason run.
