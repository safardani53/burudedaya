Home Depot is selling sleek $43,000 'shrink-proof' tiny home with a roof deck option and can be deli

TINY home fans have taken to social media to talk about The Home Depot's exciting new tiny home offering.

The $43,000 tiny home has garnered a lot of traction on Instagram - as social media users look for alternative living solutions.

The Home Depot framing kit - which only includes the home's steel structure - costs a total of $43,832.

Buyers can finance the home kit in six monthly payments - customers who chose this option will pay around $7,000 a month for half a year.

The Home Depot calls the kit a Getaway Pad. The company says it offers upgrades to furnish the home, including appliances, electric wiring, plumbing, and a second-floor terrace.

Buyers will also have to supply the foundation for the home.

The Home Depot suggests buyers should build on a level concrete slab.

The company claims that the frame's steel design makes it termite and shrink-proof.

Once the home is fully built and features are put in, buyers can enjoy its spacious, airy design.

INSIDE THE HOME

The home is designed to optimize outdoor space.

Most read in Money

Large, modern windows fill the home's interior with natural light.

Even the home's main egress is a full-length sliding glass door.

The Home Depot's model home for the framing kit features a full bathroom and a large bedroom.

The bedroom appears large enough to fit a queen-sized bed.

The model - complete with the second-floor terrace upgrade - has a spiral staircase on the side of the home.

The second-story add-on features a small half-bathroom and kitchen area.

The home was recognized on Instagram in a now-viral reel.

SOCIAL MEDIA RESPONSE

A social media personality, Adam (@adamdcmedia), posted a video about the tiny home design.

"I am so close to getting a mortgage with Home Depot," one Instagram user said.

"I'm going to buy 30 acres of land with all of my best friends and just live out there. Look at that staircase!"

The video now has over 196,000 likes.

Commenters in the video pointed out that the frame still leaves a lot of work for the home buyer.

"The listing is just for a steel frame. No siding, windows, electrical, appliances. It's JUST the frame," one commenter reminded viewers.

"That could be built with wood and two handymen in less than a week and at 10 percent of the cost."
