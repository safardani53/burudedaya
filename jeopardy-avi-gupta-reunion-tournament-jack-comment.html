Jeopardy! fans in shock as student contestant Avi talks smack to rival before wild comeback in

A JEOPARDY! contestant has thrown a little dirt at a rival player during the currently airing High School Reunion Tournament.

Stanford senior Avi Gupta shaded Northwestern senior Jack Izzo after getting a Daily Double and before making a huge comeback to win.

Mayim Bialik, 47, is hosting the 14-episode High School Reunion competition featuring 27 former Teen Tournament contestants who duked it out in 2019.

Avi from Portland, Oregon, faced Jack from San Diego, California, and Alison Purcell, a senior at the University of Texas at Austin from Bellaire, Texas.

Now older and wiser, the winner will get $100,000 and a slot in the 2023 Tournament of Champions.

Avi won that 2019 tournament and in a wild moment upon his return on Tuesday, made a dig at his opponent leaving Jeopardy! fans running for the remotes.

'NOT GONNA GET RID OF ME THAT EASY'

The summer-suited Jack led for the first half of the game when Avi landed on a Daily Double, he had $7400, Jack $16K, and Alison $10.4K

"Avi you're in third place with $7400," host Mayim remarked.

“I’m all in Mayim,” Avi sternly said, betting the house.

The clue read: "This strong, lightweight metal was named for the dozen children of Gaia & Uranus."

Avi got it right as “titanium” and then pumped his fist leaping to $14.8K.

He then turned to Jack at the middle podium and quietly said: “Not gonna get rid of me that easy.”

“Well done,” Jack could be heard politely replying.

After converting on the Daily Double, Avi went into Final Jeopardy with $16,800, Jack still leading with $19.2K, and Alison $10,600.

Under the category "Current World Leaders" it read: "In office from 2022, the president of this country has taken so many foreign trips a play on his name is 'Ferdinand Magellan Jr.'"

Only Avi was correct with "The Philippines," - The leader referred to in the clue is Ferdinand "Bongbong" Marcos Jr.

Jack said "Ukraine" which was incorrect - Avi added $4,500 to advance to the semi-finals with $21,300 in quite the comeback.

KEN TO SUB BACK IN SOON

Over the summer and before Season 39, Ken and Mayim were announced as the permanent replacements for the late longtime host, Alex Trebek who tragically passed away from pancreatic cancer at age 80 in 2020, leaving huge shoes to fill.

It took two scandal-filled years to fill them, and first Mayim was tapped and Ken soon after - but not before the show chose Mike Richards, a former Executive Producer from the inside who had made sexist comments.

Producers revealed today that Ken Jennings, 48, will be back after Mayim's three-week tournament.

"Reigning champ Stephen Webb will return, along with Ken Jennings on March 10th in syndication," they said on the podcast Inside Jeopardy!.

"We're looking forward to seeing if Stephen's streak can continue!"

Ken's popularity as the show's host led to a Twitter account called @IsKenHosting which is tracking his onscreen whereabouts - and they claimed he'll be back for another month.

The aptly named Twitter account wrote: "Another reminder that Ken Jennings will *not* be hosting Jeopardy! starting next Monday, Feb. 20 through Thursday, Mar. 9 as the show airs its High School Reunion Tournament.

He'll return on Friday, Mar. 10 and that host stint is expected to last through at least April 14."

This is welcome news for Ken-leaning legions as many were fearing that after five enjoyable months he'd be off until next season, or September 2023.

Season 39 wraps up in July 2023, so both Ken and Mayim would have hosted for roughly five months each if Mayim hosts for the remainder of the season, which is why fans were reaching an inflection point.

The scheduling news also breaks the precedent the co-hosts need to evenly split hosting duties set last season, and could be an early indicator that Mayim will mostly stick to specials going forward.

After Mayim returned to Jeopardy! this week for the latest tournament, some of the show's spirited fans took to Twitter, Instagram, and Facebook to flat-out complain.

After Monday's debut, they criticized a contestant being awarded points for a seemingly incorrect answer.

Under the category "National Anthems," the clue read: "Heard here is this British national anthem that has the same melody as 'My Country, 'Tis Of Thee.'"

College senior Audrey Sarin responded: "What is God Save the Queen," which Bialik accepted as the correct answer, leading to a debate online.

"Unless this was taped before the Queen passed the answer is God Save the King," one viewer tweeted, in reference to Britain's Queen Elizabeth II's death in September, with King Charles III becoming the monarch.

Elsewhere, others objected to the return of Mayim full-stop, with one viewer writing on Twitter: "Three weeks of Mayim Bialik on Jeopardy feels like I'm being punished for something. I probably deserve it but I'm still not happy about it."

The sitcom actress was originally going to switch in for winningest contestant Ken in December, but execs earlier revealed her schedule "evolved" given her Call Me Kat sitcom which has seemingly been her priority.
