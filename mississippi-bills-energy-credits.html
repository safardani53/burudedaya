Americans can save $300 a year on energy bills - plus score $1,000 energy credits, see who can apply

IN response to higher energy bills, Entergy Mississippi is offering $3.2million in aid to customers.

The company recently launched what's called Operation Bill Assist to offset the rising natural gas costs, which have more than doubled.

Additionally, Entergy Mississippi is giving away money-saving, energy-efficiency kits totaling $1.1million.

Robbie Kemp, Entergy Mississippi vice president of customer service said in a statement, “These kits are available to all residential customers online, while supplies last, and can potentially save a household significant money on their electric bill.”

Mr. Kemp also mentioned that if customers use both the kits and company online savings tools, they can save up to $300 a year.

Customers are encouraged to order the free energy kits at https://msentergysolutions-marketplace.com/.

Each kit comes with the following:

  • Four standard LED bulbs
  • Two specialty LED bulbs
  • One advanced power strip
  • One LED night light
  • Two bathroom faucet aerators
  • 17 feet of V-seal weatherstripping

Operation Bill Assist

This program aims to assist all residential customers but will focus on those with low to moderate incomes.

Entergy Mississippi will provide temporary assistance from a $1.1million pot.

This will be for moderate-income customers struggling to pay utility bills but do not qualify for federal bill assistance programs.

According to the company, this program provided over 7,300 customers with a one-time $150 bill credit.

Additionally, over $540,000 will be allotted to those with emergency utility bill payments.

Funds up to $1,000 will be issued to older adults and customers with disabilities facing financial hardship.

Just note that the payments are temporary and may only be given for one or more months.

Other energy assistance

Michigan's public utility Consumers Energy recently announced $7.25million in funding that will be distributed to eight Michigan nonprofits across the state.

According to the company, customers facing hardship or trying to access support for the new funding are eligible.

To receive the aid just call 2-1-1, a free resource connecting Michiganders with nonprofits for overall basic needs, including assistance with energy bills.

The company says customers may also try 800-477-5050 if they are facing challenges with their bill.

Plus, New York's “Emergency HEAP Benefit” applications start Tuesday, January 3.

This program aims to help needy residents heat their homes in light of an emergency.

The payment amount for those eligible ranges from $185 to $900 for 2022-2023.

Eligibility is determined by a number of factors including income, household size, and more.

Additionally, benefit amounts depend on the type of heat emergency sustained.

For questions about HEAP, you can reach out through your county’s contact page.
