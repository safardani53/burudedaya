Greg Rousseau | Early Life & Family

Gregory Rousseau was born in Coconut Creek, Florida, on April 5, 2000. He is a Haitian immigrant’s son. His father’s name is Oskal Rousseau, and his mother’s name is Anne Rousseau.

Greg’s parents, Anne and Oscal, did not meet in Haiti, though. They happened to cross paths walking down a Brooklyn street in opposite directions as teenagers.

His parents moved to Western New York to continue their education. In 1995, they exchanged vows at Buffalo City Hall.

He once claimed that he was familiar with Buffalo because his parents spent four years there.

Oscal attended ECC and worked as a cab driver, while Anne attended Buffalo Vocational Technical Center and graduated as a licensed practical nurse.

Her first job was working the night shift at the nursing home in Kenmore called McAuley Residence.

Anne gave birth to their second child and Greg’s little brother Marvyn at Sisters of Charity Hospital in 1996. 

After graduating from nursing school, Anne worked in the intensive care unit at Florida Medical Center in Fort Lauderdale.

Rousseau attended Champa Catholic High School in Hialeah, Florida. He then committed to playing college football at Miami University.

The player took his academics seriously and was a brilliant student.

Greg Rousseau | Professional playing career

The Buffalo Bills selected Rousseau in the first round of the 2021 NFL Draft, 30th overall. On June 4, 2021, he agreed to an $11.37 million, four-year rookie contract. 

Greg was nominated for NFL Rookie of the Week honors in Week 2 of the 2021 season for his five tackles, two sacks, and two tackles made behind the line of scrimmage.

Similarly, on October 10, 2021, Rousseau made his first career interception in a 38-20 Week 5 Sunday night victory over Patrick Mahomes and the Kansas City Chiefs. 

The player won the AFC Defensive Player of the Week in the process.
