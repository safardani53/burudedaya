MORE ON HER DIAGNOSIS

A cranial hematoma, or intracranial hematoma, is a "collection of blood within the skull," according to Mayo Clinic.

"It is usually caused by a blood vessel that bursts in the brain."

The website went on to say that the condition can be caused by trauma or a fall.

Some symptoms include headaches, dizziness, confusion, slurred speech, and vomiting.

Cedars-Sinai classifies a craniectomy as a "surgery to remove a portion of someone's skull to relieve extra pressure on the brain."
