Is Chaunce Jenkins, Ja Morant Brother? Are They Related Or Just Lookalike?

Chaunce Jenkins isn’t Ja Morant’s brother. The two basketball players aren’t related to one another; they are just lookalikes with similar playing styles.

The internet buzzed excitedly when basketball fans noticed a college player who looked just like Ja Morant, sparking rumors that they might be related.

Chaunce Jenkins, born on April 20, 2002, is a basketball player (guard) who plays for the Old Dominion Monarchs.

Is Chaunce Jenkins, Ja Morant Brother?

The 21-year-old basketball player isn’t Ja Morant brother. The buzz about them being siblings started when fans spotted someone who looked a lot like Ja.

In June 2023, Morant faced a 25-game suspension from the NBA due to some controversies.

The NBA fans were shocked when they saw Ja playing college basketball. Later, sports enthusiasts discovered it wasn’t Morant on the court but his lookalike, Chaunce Jenkins.

The uncanny resemblance initially sparked confusion and surprise in the basketball community.

Jenkins doesn’t just resemble Morant; his basketball style mirrors Morant’s in many ways.

While Jenkins and Morant are not brothers, the only commonality between them lies in their similar approach to the game. Nonetheless, both Chaunce and Ja have their own respective siblings.

Morant plays professional basketball as a point guard for the Memphis Grizzlies of the NBA.

The three-time All-Region Most Valuable Player played basketball at Crestwood High School.

The 2020 NBA Rookie of the Year winner played college basketball for the Murray State Racers.

During his sophomore year in college, Morant made NCAA history by being the first player ever to average over 20 points and more than 10 assists per game in a single season.

Following college career, the 2022 NBA Most Improved Player Award winner was selected by the Grizzlies with the second overall pick in the 2019 NBA draft.

Ja Morant Has A Younger Sister Named Teniya Morant

Teniya also plays basketball as a point guard, like her older sibling. She played high school basketball at Houston High School.

In her last year of high school basketball, she scored an average of 11.2 points per game, grabbed 4.7 rebounds, made 1.4 assists, and secured 1.5 steals.

In May 2023, she announced her commitment to play at Mississippi Valley State University.

She chose MVSU after they offered her a scholarship in January, which was her first chance to play Division I basketball. This offer influenced her decision to go to college.

Chaunce Jenkins Has Two Siblings
