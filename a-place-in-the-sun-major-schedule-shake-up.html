A Place in the Sun viewers all say the same thing after major schedule shake up to Channel 4 show

A PLACE in the Sun fans were left shocked after Channel 4 aired a double episode of the property show in a schedule shake-up.

Viewers were surprised when an episode of the Channel 4 property series aired at 3pm on Monday and another episode in its usual slot at 4pm.

It led to confusion online as fans called out the broadcaster for switching up the schedule.

Taking to Twitter, one said: "Wait, what?! Two episodes back-to-back? This timeslot is getting so confusing. Afternoon placers, first time today I'm not on a call."

Another wrote: "Double episode?? What is this witchcraft?"

A third penned: "WTF a double bill. The world has gone to pot."

A fourth commented: "Have we had double helpings of this today?"

The series is currently presented by an array of on-screen talent including Jasmine Harman, Laura Hamilton and Jean Johansson.

Its previous stars include Jonnie Irwin, who famously claimed the show dumped him as a result of his terminal illness, Scarlette Douglas and Danni Menzies.

Presenter Amanda Lamb has teased a possible return to the property TV show after quitting 14 years ago.

Amanda, 50, fronted the show from 2001 until 2009.

Speaking to Entertainment Daily, Amanda revealed: "Rumours are I might be going back to do a few episodes so watch this space.

"I can’t tell you any more because it’s a secret but I might be doing some. You’ll have to wait and see!"

Amanda said she had got a taste of what it was like to go back on the road while filming the Holiday Home Show.

There has been no announcement about when Amanda will return to host A Place in the Sun.

A Place in the Sun airs weekdays at 4pm on Channel 4.
