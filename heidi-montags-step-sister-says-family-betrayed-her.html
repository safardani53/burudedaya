Heidi Montag's step-sister relives sexual abuse at hands of star's dad &amp; says 'she betrayed me by no

HEIDI Montag’s step-sister - who suffered horrific sexual abuse at the hands of the star's dad - says she feels betrayed she never believed her and remained close to him, The Sun can exclusively reveal.

The Hills star's father Bill was convicted of child abuse back in 2016, and her step-sister Carissa O'Hara Berlet later came forward as his victim.

Bill is Heidi and sister Holly's biological father, who split from their mom Darlene in 1990 before remarrying Terri O'Hara, Carissa's mother.

The 74-year-old was arrested in 2015 over child abuse claims which allegedly took place between 1993 and 1998, when Carissa was a young teen living with her mother and stepfather at different homes in Colorado.

Bill initially pleaded not guilty, before changing his plea for a lesser charge of child abuse without injury.

Due to the statute of limitations, he was only eligible to be charged with crimes that occurred after July 1 1996, when Carissa would have been 16 years old.

Though she was not named in the court case, she has since come forward and told The Sun in a new interview she was "terrified" of telling her family - who she feels let her down.

After revealing her trauma to a therapist before Bill’s arrest, Carissa alleged she endured more than 50 instances of abuse over time.

In her younger years, she struggled with substance abuse issues and attempted suicide, but has now recovered and is moving on with her life.

Speaking exclusively to The Sun, she said: "I have no desire to reconnect with Heidi or Holly.

"I have decided that it is healthier for me to not surround myself with people that do not believe I was assaulted by Bill.

"I believe it is sad that they outwardly support victims in the MeToo movement and yet [do] not support their sister.

"I am not sure what I would say to them now. I believe they know the truth now but aren't brave enough to live in it."

Discussing her terrifying experience with Bill, she claims the abuse took place in Frisco, Centennial, and Steamboat Springs Colorado from halfway through her 8th grade year through high school.

"We lived in three different homes during that time. [The abuse] was kept very private," she said.

"Heidi and Holly would be there on weekends, but I was there all the time.

"Bill didn't have to threaten me with silence. He knew that I was terrified. It took me years to gain the strength to come forward.

"My husband and my three kids have been incredibly supportive. As have my friends that stepped up and supported me. And have become family.

"'I support you!’ ‘I believe you!’ These two short statements carry so much power and so much importance to a victim of sexual assault. And yet, too few victims get to hear these words.

"The MeToo movement has been near and dear to my heart since I came forward with my own MeToo experience in late 2013.

"Coming forward, I desperately wanted to hear these words from my family.

"I have never had a conversation with anyone in my family, except my husband and age appropriate talks with my kids, about the abuse.

"I was told when the investigation started in 2014 that they had made up their minds, they didn't believe me and nothing would change that."

Carissa was left heartbroken by the fact none of the family, except her husband and a close friend, attended the court appearances after telling investigators they didn't believe her.

"I have no idea where they got their information from in order to make a decision about believing me or not, except from Bill," she said.

"I have never been given the opportunity to sit with any family and tell my side of the story; they don't want to hear it. No one wants to know.

"I don't know if it would have made a difference to at least have been able to tell my family my experience.

"It is devastating to have them turn away from the truth without even hearing my side."

The Sun has contacted Heidi's rep for comment, but did not hear back.

Her father Bill was arrested in 2015 at Aspen Valley Ski and Snowboard Club, where he worked at the time, and was suspended following the charges.

Although he was sentenced to four years of supervised probation, he reportedly completed it early in February 2019.

Heidi and her family have never spoken out about the abuse, and Carissa says they have not been in touch since 2013.

Bill walked mom-of-one Heidi, 34, down the aisle during her 2009 wedding to Spencer Pratt.

Since Colorado-mom Carissa, 40, came forward, Heidi has not shared any photographs with Bill on her Instagram, although he posted a snap on his Facebook page in 2018, marking Father's Day.

The photograph features Heidi carrying her son, Gunner, who was born the year before.

"To my knowledge, all of my family on my mom's side has continued to be in routine contact with Bill including Holly and Heidi and their kids," Carissa told The Sun.

She went on: "In the years since coming forward, I have become even more aware of the issues surrounding coming forward for victims of sexual assault.

"Based on my experience, I believe one of the biggest reasons that victims have a hard time coming forward is the lack of belief and support they endure after sharing their experiences.

"It is astounding that in the MeToo era there are still those who choose to openly deny some victims’ experiences.

"I unfortunately have experienced this intense betrayal by those closest to me; by those I expected to stand by my side, as I came forward.

"I have experienced the devastating trauma of having my family deny my experience of abuse even after my abuser was convicted.

"I walked through the entire investigation and court case against my abuser without the support of my family."

Carissa said that she was disappointed in the legal system after Bill escaped jail time, saying she wanted her day in court.

Her mother is still married to Bill, and although she hasn't cut her off, Carissa says she only speaks to her "occasionally."

She says she has "no desire" to speak to her step-father again and says she doesn't care what he's doing with his life, after being granted a permanent restraining order against him.

Talking about her sisters, Carissa told The Sun: "I, and many other victims, have had to learn to live without the love and support they deserve after living through a sexual assault.

"This is a sad fact that should not be and more needs to be done.
