Paul Desmond (Saxophonist) - Age, Birthday, Bio, Facts, Family, Net Worth, Height &amp; More

Paul Desmond is an American saxophonist and alto jazz composer, best known for his work with Dave Brubeck Quartet and composing the group's biggest hit, "Take Five". He's one of the most popular musicians in the cool jazz scene. In addition to his work with Brubeck, he has led several groups and collaborated with Gerry Mulligan, Chet Baker, Jim Hall and Ed Bickert. After years of smoking and poor health, Desmond died of lung cancer in 1977 after touring with Brubeck.
