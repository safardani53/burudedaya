George Browne Post is an American architect trained in the Fine Arts tradition. He is recognized as the master of modern American architecture and the man who publicly gave birth to skyscrapers. Many of his most iconic projects involve commercial buildings, where new demands have pushed the traditional limits of design. Many of the buildings he designed were demolished, as their central location in New York and other cities made them vulnerable to reconstruction in the 20th century. Some of its lost buildings are considered diodes. product of their time. He was active from 1869 almost until his death in 1913. His sons, who entered business in 1904, continued as George B. Post and Sons until 1930.
