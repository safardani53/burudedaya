Ken Squier Health And Illness Update: What Was His Cause Of Death?

Lately, Ken Squier’s health and illness have grabbed a lot of media and NASCAR fans’ attention. Unfortunately, Squier, the legendary broadcaster, died at 88.

As per the management of the local WDEV radio, he took his last breath on Wednesday (November 15, 2023).

The NASCAR Hall of Famer reported stock car events on television for more than three decades.

Squier would be missed in the NASCAR realm for his uncanny contributions as an announcer.

Ken Squier was more than just a sportscaster; he was a legendary figure in the world of NASCAR.

Hailing from Waterbury, Vermont, Squier left an indelible mark on the sport, serving as the lap-by-lap commentator for NASCAR.

Reportedly, he worked for CBS from 1979 to 1997 and contributed his distinctive voice to TBS from 1983 to 1999.

Beyond the microphone, Squier played a pivotal role in shaping the narrative of NASCAR. He is credited for coining the term “The Great American Race” for the Daytona 500 in 1979.

Squier always had an eagerness to deliver memorable phrases. His folksy, New England charm and natural linguistic talent made his voice an integral part of NASCAR’s modern soundtrack.

Moreover, his unique broadcasting style featured grandiose words and colorful metaphors.

Ken Squier Health Update: Cause Of Death

As with any icon, the passage of time inevitably brings health challenges. For Ken Squier, these challenges became increasingly apparent in recent years.

At the age of 88, Squier faced the ultimate adversary—an intestinal blockage that led to his untimely death.

The motorsports community is mourning the loss of a broadcasting legend who was crucial in elevating NASCAR’s profile.

Dave Moody, a longtime friend and MRN colleague, conveyed the sad news of Ken Squier’s passing on Thursday morning, November 16.

“Ken earned his wings last night at 8:20 PM ET, surrounded by his incredible, loving family,” Dave Moody wrote on X. “I grieve for the loss of my dear friend and lifelong mentor but rejoice in the fact that his pain and struggle are over.”

The confirmation came later from WDEV, Squier’s Vermont radio station, cementing the loss of a broadcasting icon.

Fans and media paid tribute to Squier on social media following his passing, recognizing his lasting impact on motorsports.

NASCAR great Dale Earnhardt Jr. also posted about the broadcaster’s death on X. “I’m convinced that race would not have had its lasting impact had Ken not been our lead narrator,” he said.

Ken Squier Was Suffering From A Series Of Health Challenges

The last three years of Ken Squier’s life were marked by a series of health challenges that tested his resilience.

A battle with shingles proved to be a formidable opponent, followed by a diagnosis of COVID-19 in 2021, which exacerbated his already deteriorating health due to old age.

Despite the severity of the pandemic, Squier fought valiantly, ultimately recovering well from the virus.

However, the challenges persisted as he endured a minor stroke and later a fractured pelvis.

Ken Squier faced these hurdles with a brave heart and a determination to overcome them.

Despite the setbacks, he was on a trajectory toward full recovery, proving the indomitable spirit that defined his illustrious career.

Unfortunately, the intestinal blockage that emerged in the final days of his life presented an insurmountable obstacle to the frailty of his aging body.

In a poignant turn of events, Squier was moved out of the hospice facility on Wednesday, choosing to spend his remaining time at home surrounded by loved ones.
