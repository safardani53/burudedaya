Early Life & Family

Delon Reginald Wright, popularly known as Delon Wright. He was born in Los Angeles, California, on April 26, 1992. Thus, he is 31 years old and belongs to the black ethnicity.

 His father is Ray Wright, and his mother is Stacy Adams Wright. He grew up with his only sibling Dorell Wright.

His brother, Dorell Wright, is an American former professional basketball player who had played for the Miami Heat, Golden State Warriors, Philadelphia 76ers, and Portland Trail Blazers of the NBA.

The two Wright brothers have an almost age difference of seven years. Their parents kept the brothers involved in athletes from an early age.

Dorell attended Leuzinger high school and was drafted by Miami Heat in the 2004 NBA Draft. In addition, he also won the 2006 NBA championship with Miami Heat.

Basketball Career

High School Career

Delon Wright attended Leuzinger High School in Lawndale, California. As a senior, Wright helped lead Leuzinger High School to the California State Championship in 2011.

Thus, Delon Wright was named CIF Southern Section Division 1A Player of the Year. He attended the California College Athletic Association (CCCAA) School at the City College of San Francisco.

After two successful seasons with college, Wright committed to Utah over Washington, Gonzaga, Washington State, and Saint Mary’s.

College Career

After he came in for Utah in his first season, Delon Wright instantly became one of the top players in the Pac-12 Conference.

He completed in the top 10 in the Pac-12 Conference; he averaged 16.9 points, 4.89 steals, and 2.56 blocks, with a field goal percentage of 56.1 percent and a free-throw percentage of 80.5.

To sum up, Delon Wright became the first Ute player to be named first-team All-Pac-12 and labeled the league’s All-Defensive team.

In the meantime, Delon Wright was regarded entering the 2014 NBA Draft but eventually decided to return to Utah for his senior season.

Senior Season

At the beginning of Wright’s senior season, Wright was named to the 50-man watch list for the Naismith College Player of the Year and the John R. Wooden Award.

He was voted first-team All-Pac-12 and named the Pac-12 All-Defensive Team for the second-straight season.

Delon Wright was also a two-time member of the Pac-12 All-Defensive team and gained the 2015 Bob Cousy Award.

Wright became the first player in Utah Men’s basketball history to be labeled the first-team All-Pac-12 in two consecutive years in 2014 and 2015.

Professional Career

Toronto Raptors

Delon Wright was drafted with the 20th overall pick in the 2015 NBA Draft by the Toronto Raptors on June 25, 2015.

He signed his rookie contract with the Toronto Raptors and was subsequently enlisted in the team for the 2015 NBA Summer League.

In April 2016, Wright scored a season-high 19 points in a 111-98 victory against the Indiana Pacers. He had multiple stints with Raptors 905 of the NBA Development League.

Wright missed the 2016-17 season due to a right shoulder injury. In January 2018, Wright scored a career-high 25 points and 13 rebounds in a 124-115 victory over the Chicago Bulls.

Also, he had his first career double-double and struck four 3-pointers to dominate the second unit.

Memphis Grizzlies

Delon Wright was traded to Memphis Grizzlies for Marc Gasol on February 7, 2019.

Then, on April 5, Wright scored a career-high 26 points in a 122-112 win against the Dallas Mavericks. In addition, he also recorded his first career triple-double and added 14 assists and ten rebounds.

Afterward, Wright recorded his second triple-double and added 20 points, 12 assists, and 13 rebounds in a 129-127 win against the Dallas Mavericks.

Following that, Wright logged his third triple-double and scored 13 points, 11 rebounds, and 11 assists in a 132-117 victory over the Golden State Warriors.

Dallas Mavericks

In July 2019, Delon Wright was procured by the Dallas Mavericks in a sign-and-trade deal with the Memphis Grizzlies in a swap for a two-second-round pick and the draft rights to Satnam Singh.

Detroit Pistons

In November 2020, Wright was traded to the Detroit Pistons in a three-team trade, including the Oklahoma City Thunder.

In January 2021, Wright made a career-high 28 points in a 119-104 victory against the Philadelphia 76ers.

Sacramento Kings

In March 2021, Delon Wright was traded to the Sacramento Kings for Cory Joseph and two future second-round draft picks.

Hence, Delon only played with the Sacramento Kings for roughly three months.

Recent Contracts

In August 2021, Wright was traded to the Atlanta Hawks in a three-team trade involving the Boston Celtics. 

On July 6, 2022, after the Hawks, Wright signed 2 years, $16 million contract with Washington Wizards.

Wright Legacy Foundation

The Wright Legacy Foundation was inaugurated by brothers Dorell Wright and Delon Wright, also Mia Wright (wife of Dorell Wright), in 2017.

The Wright Legacy Foundation was set up to promote the development of young people dwelling in California through mentorship and high-level guidelines.

Thus, the foundation is intended to achieve high-level college placement percentages within colleges and universities throughout the country.

As it is hard for some residents to pay for higher education and overcome hardship, the Wright Legacy Scholarship Fund was established to contribute and significantly impact the lives of youth.
