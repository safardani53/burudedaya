Fela Kuti (Saxophonist) - Age, Birthday, Bio, Facts, Family, Net Worth, Height &amp; More

Fela Anikulapo Kuti is a Nigerian Pan-African, multi-instrumentalist, band, songwriter, political activist and activist. He is considered the pioneer of Afrobeat, an African musical genre that combines traditional Yoruba and Afro-Cuban music with funk and jazz. At the height of his popularity, he was considered one of Africa's most “inspiring and charismatic” musical artists. AllMusic describes him as a “musical and political social voice” of international significance. Kuti is the son of Nigerian women's rights activist, Funmilayo Ransome-Kuti. After initial experiences abroad, he and his group Africa 70 became stars in Nigeria in the 1970s, during which time he was an open critic and a target of the Nigerian military. In 1970, he founded the Republican Municipality of Kalakuta, which declared its independence from the military regime. The town was destroyed in a raid in 1978. Since his death in 1997, his re-distribution and compilation of music have been overseen by his son, Femi Kuti.
